Our philosophy

Structure left visible is a promise kept to the client.

Most homes hide their bones behind drywall. We build the frame first, in whole timbers cut and joined by hand, and then design the rest of the house around letting you see it. It's a slower, costlier way to build — and it's the only one we do.

Every project starts on the site itself: the slope, the tree line, the direction the light comes from in December. The house follows from there, not from a catalogue plan.

What we take on

Three kinds of work, each led by the same two principals from first sketch to final walkthrough.

Full builds

New timber homes

Ground-up residences on rural and coastal sites, typically 1,800–4,200 sq ft, built on a 12–18 month timeline.

Additions

Extending existing homes

Timber-frame additions — sunrooms, second storeys, studios — matched to a home's existing structural logic.

Advisory

Owner-builder consulting

Structural drawings and site visits for clients managing their own build, without full studio oversight.

How a project moves

Four stages, roughly a year end to end for a full build. Nothing is subcontracted out of order.

01

Site & brief

Two site visits, a survey, and a written brief covering budget, use, and how you actually live.

02

Frame design

The structural frame is drawn first — it sets the rooms, not the other way around.

03

Raising

Timbers are joined off-site, then raised in a matter of days once the foundation is ready.

04

Close-in & finish

Envelope, systems, and interior finishing, with the frame left exposed wherever the plan allows.
